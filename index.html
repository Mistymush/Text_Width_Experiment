Experiment: Text Width

The following web-page contains two visualisations of a data set collected
from 140 playthroughs of the puzzle game Eukaryote, designed by an MQP team at WPI.

Players raced to complete 8 puzzles within a fifteen minute time limit.
Unknowingly, players were assigned one of two tutorial level progressions shown above.

Roughly half of players received the one level depicted on the right.
Those who received the second longer tutorial, first played the two easy levels depicted
on the left, and then proceeded to the same level as the other players depicted on the right.

During the experiment, player statistics of performance on each level were recorded alongside
a video recording of each player's face to used for AI analysis.

From this data the team has generated two hypotheses about puzzle gameplay:

1: The long level progression provides a smoother learning experience.
2: During gameplay, player's emotions cycle from being frustrated for longer periods
to being joyful for a short period when the completing a level.

The following Vis attempts to answer the first question:
Is one level progression easier to learn than another?

In each scatter plot blue represents players who received the longer tutorial, these players
started the game on level -2.
Players who received the longer tutorial can be viewed as orange starting at level 0.
